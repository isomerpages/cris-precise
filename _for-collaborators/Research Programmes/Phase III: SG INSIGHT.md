<p>Phase III of the National Precision Medicine (NPM) programme marks a pivotal
moment where scientific advances are translated from pilot to population
scale. The ambition has expanded in both scale and scope, including up
to 450,000 residents receiving medical treatments and healthcare services.</p>
<p>While Phase II focused on healthy volunteers, Phase III shifts to patient
cohorts across all three of Singapore’s healthcare clusters: NHG Health,
NUHS, and SingHealth. Studying patients in real-world settings allows the
programme to address the entire healthcare life-course, from early to late-stage
disease.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Furthermore, the falling prices of genetic sequencing mean that it may
soon make economic and clinical sense for each patient’s entire genome
to be sequenced once and analysed as needed across their lifetime, a concept
known as sequence once, analyse repeatedly (SOAR).</p>
<p></p>
<h3><strong>Phase III Goals</strong></h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Generate whole genome sequences of about 10% of Singapore’s resident population.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Establish Genomic Innovation Hubs in each healthcare cluster to identify
genetic conditions that may be suitable for eventual mainstreaming</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Prototype pharmacogenomics to demonstrate large-scale whole genome sequencing
clinical utility</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enrich PRECISE-SG100K (Phase II) dataset with new multi-omic, imaging
and digital phenotyping data, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Build a data platform connecting genomic, clinical, and phenotypic data
to support research and clinical use</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Build a community of engaged patient participants who actively contribute
to genomic research and healthcare innovation</p>
